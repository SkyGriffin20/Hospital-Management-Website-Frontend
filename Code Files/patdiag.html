Diagnosis: Chronic Rhinosinusitis with Postnasal Drip


Chronic rhinosinusitis (CRS) is a persistent inflammation of the nasal passages and sinuses lasting for 12 weeks or longer despite medical treatment. It often presents with symptoms such as nasal congestion, postnasal drip, facial pressure, and sore throat. Postnasal drip, where excess mucus drips down the back of the throat, can cause irritation and inflammation, leading to a sore throat.
